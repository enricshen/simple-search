Territorial Local Authorities (city or district councils) are responsible for essential community services, such as:

  • consents for subdivisions, commercial/residential buildings
  • local road maintenance (excluding state highways)
  • water, sewerage, stormwater, rubbish collection services
  • community services like libraries, swimming pools and pulic parks

Regional Council are responsible for natural resources and economic development in the region, such as:

  • looking after freshwater, land, air and coastal water
  • managing rivers, mitigating soil erosion and flood control
  • regional emergency management and civil defence preparedness.
  • regional transport planning
  • Harbour navigation and safety, oil spills and other marine pollution

A unitary authority has both the responsibilities of a local council and a regional council.

There are six unitary authorities in New Zealand: Auckland Council, Gisborne District Council, Chatham Islands Council, Nelson City Council, Marlborough District Council and Tasman District Council.
